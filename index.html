Jaime Merino Strategy

Multi-timeframe momentum + trend confluence system

SqFlow v1.4

This system identifies high-probability entries by requiring 5 simultaneous confluence conditions across trend, momentum, volatility, and volume. A trade is only signalled when all five align — filtering out noise and keeping you on the right side of the dominant trend.

The Dashboard shows a Confluence Score out of 5. A full score (5/5) is required before entering a trade. Each condition represents one pillar of the strategy.

1 EMA Trend Bias Trend

What it checks: Price must be above EMA55 (or within 3% of it) and EMA55 must be above EMA200 for a long signal. The reverse applies for shorts.

Why it matters: EMA55 defines the medium-term bias. EMA200 defines the macro trend. Trading in the direction of both EMAs means you're aligned with institutions and swing traders — dramatically improving your win rate.

What to look for: GREEN when price ≥ EMA55 and EMA55 > EMA200. Ideal setup is a bounce off EMA55 on a pullback.

2 ADX Trend Strength Strength

What it checks: ADX(14) must be above 23 with a rising slope (increasing over the last 3 bars).

Why it matters: ADX measures how strong a trend is, not its direction. A reading above 23 confirms the market is trending (not choppy). The rising slope requirement means the trend is accelerating — the optimal moment to enter.

What to look for: GREEN when ADX > 23 and rising. Values 25–40 are ideal. Above 40 the trend may be overextended. Below 20 = choppy, avoid trading.

3 RSI Not Overextended Momentum

What it checks: RSI(14) must be below 70 for longs (not overbought) or above 30 for shorts (not oversold).

Why it matters: Entering when RSI is at an extreme means the move may already be exhausted. This filter keeps you from chasing. The ideal RSI for a long entry is 45–65 — strong but not overheated.

What to look for: GREEN when RSI is below 70 (long) or above 30 (short). RED means the market is overextended — wait for a reset.

4 Squeeze Momentum Volatility

What it checks: The TTM Squeeze indicator must have fired (released from squeeze) or have positive/rising momentum histogram.

Why it matters: The TTM Squeeze detects when Bollinger Bands contract inside Keltner Channels — a "coiling" of volatility. When the squeeze fires (BBs expand outside KCs), price is ready to make a big directional move. This is the volatility catalyst that powers the entry.

What to look for: GREEN when squeeze has fired or momentum is positive. The most powerful setups occur right as the squeeze fires after a long compression period.

5 Price at Key Level / Vol POC Volume

What it checks: Price must be within 2.5% of the Volume Point of Control (VOL POC) or a key support/resistance level.

Why it matters: The VOL POC is the price level with the highest traded volume in the lookback window. Price gravitates toward this level and often reacts strongly from it — it acts as a magnet and a high-probability bounce zone.

What to look for: GREEN when price is within 2.5% of the VOL POC. Best entries occur when price pulls back to this level while all other conditions are met.

The confluence approach eliminates most false signals. A single indicator can be wrong; five aligned indicators rarely are.

The Live Indicator Readings card shows real-time computed values for all 9 indicators used in the strategy. Here's what each means:

EMA 10 Fast Trend

Calculation: Exponential Moving Average of the last 10 candle closes. More weight is given to recent candles.

Purpose: Short-term momentum filter. When EMA10 crosses above EMA55, it signals short-term bullish momentum aligning with the medium-term bias.

In this strategy: Used to confirm that fast price action is in sync with the trend. EMA10 > EMA55 = bullish micro-momentum.

EMA 55 Bias Core

Calculation: Exponential Moving Average of the last 55 candle closes. Reacts faster than the 200 EMA but slower than EMA10.

Purpose: The primary trend bias indicator. Jaime Merino uses EMA55 as the "line in the sand" — price above it = bullish bias, price below = bearish bias.

In this strategy: Acts as a dynamic support/resistance level. Pullbacks to EMA55 in an uptrend are ideal entry zones (the "bounce"). Within 3% of EMA55 counts as valid.

EMA 200 Macro Filter

Calculation: Exponential Moving Average of the last 200 candle closes. Slow-moving, rarely changes direction quickly.

Purpose: The macro trend filter. Institutional traders and funds watch the EMA200 closely. Price above EMA200 = bull market structure; below = bear market structure.

In this strategy: EMA55 must be above EMA200 for longs (and below for shorts). This ensures you're trading with the big picture, not against it.

RSI (14) Oscillator Momentum

Calculation: Relative Strength Index over 14 periods. Compares average gains vs average losses, normalized to a 0–100 scale.

Purpose: Measures whether price is overbought (>70) or oversold (<30). The strategy uses it as a filter, not a trigger.

In this strategy: RSI must be below 70 for longs (room to run) and above 30 for shorts. Ideal long entry zone: RSI between 45–65. This prevents chasing extended moves.

> 70 = Overbought (avoid longs) 45–65 = Ideal long zone < 30 = Oversold (avoid shorts)
ADX (14) Strength Trend

Calculation: Average Directional Index over 14 periods. Derived from +DI and −DI directional indicators. Measures trend strength, not direction.

Purpose: Confirms the market is actually trending (not ranging/choppy). A trend-following strategy only works when there IS a trend.

In this strategy: ADX must exceed 23 AND be rising (slope positive over 3 bars). The rising requirement is key — it means the trend is strengthening, not weakening.

< 20 = No trend (choppy) 20–23 = Weak trend (borderline) > 23 ↑ = Valid (trending + accelerating) > 40 = Strong but possibly overextended
BB Upper & BB Lower Bands Volatility

Calculation: Bollinger Bands = 20-period SMA ± 2 standard deviations. Upper band = SMA + 2σ, Lower band = SMA − 2σ.

Purpose: Measure price volatility and identify overextension. Bands expand in high volatility and contract in low volatility (squeeze).

In this strategy: Used as part of the Squeeze calculation. When BBs contract inside Keltner Channels, a squeeze is forming. BB Upper and Lower values shown as reference price levels for gauging how extended price is relative to the band envelope.

Squeeze TTM Volatility

Calculation: TTM Squeeze by John Carter. Compares Bollinger Bands width to Keltner Channel width. When BBs are inside KCs = ON (squeeze active). When BBs expand outside KCs = FIRED (momentum release). Histogram = linear regression of momentum.

Purpose: Identifies periods of volatility compression followed by explosive breakouts. The squeeze is like a coiled spring — the longer the compression, the more powerful the release.

In this strategy: Condition 4 checks if the squeeze has fired or if momentum histogram is positive/rising. Best entries: squeeze fires in the direction of the EMA trend bias.

ON = Volatility compressed, stand by FIRED ↑ = Breakout in progress — look to enter FIRED ↓ = Breakdown in progress
Vol POC Volume Profile Volume

Calculation: Volume Point of Control — the price level where the most volume was traded in the last 300 candles. Calculated by dividing the price range into buckets and summing volume at each level.

Purpose: High-volume price levels act as magnets — price frequently returns to them and reacts (bounces or breaks) from them. The POC represents "fair value" in the eyes of the market.

In this strategy: Condition 5 checks if price is within 2.5% of the VOL POC. Trading near the POC increases the probability of a clean reaction. Ideal setup: price pulls back to POC while all trend and momentum conditions are met.

The trade parameter card calculates your position sizing and levels automatically based on your capital input and the Jaime Merino risk rules.

Position Size
10% of Capital
Never risk your entire account on one trade. 10% keeps any single loss manageable and allows you to take multiple concurrent positions.
Stop Loss
−7% from Entry
A fixed 7% stop loss from entry price. This gives the trade room to breathe while defining your maximum loss before the thesis is invalidated.
Take Profit
+21% from Entry
A 1:3 risk-reward ratio. For every dollar risked, you target 3 dollars in profit. This means you only need to be right ~33% of the time to break even.
Partial Scale-Out
+14% (1:2 R/R)
Optional: close 50% of the position at 1:2 R/R (+14%) to lock in profit and reduce risk on the remaining position to breakeven.
Max Loss per Trade
With 10% position size and 7% stop loss, your maximum loss per trade is 0.7% of total capital. This keeps drawdowns controlled even on losing streaks.
Leverage
Calculated automatically based on your capital. Leverage amplifies both gains and losses — always ensure your stop loss is set before entering.
